Students belonging to different income groups world wide

The analysis shows that 33% of university students come from High income families,
27% from lower middle income families, 37% from upper middle income families, and the remaining from low income groups.
The exact reasons for these enrollment percentages are unknown and could be due to various factors such as accessibility of education, financial aid, cultural attitudes, economic conditions, and university-specific factors. Students Attending Public or Private Universities From 1950 - 2020

In our dataset, university were classified as public or private. So we counted the total number of students that attended private and public school every year. On hovering on each of the bar, we can also see the percentage of each group for every year. It looks that the percentage of students attending public schools increased from 36% to 44% from 1950 to 2020. The main reason for this trend is the fact that public colleges are government-funded, making easily accesible by students of every income group; while private schools rely more on tuition and endowments.

Year-wise Trend On Income Groups

The stack bar chart indicates that from 1950 to 2020, the percentage of university students from the upper middle income group has increased, while the percentage from the lower income group has remained low. This trend could be due to a variety of factors such as improved accessibility to education for upper middle income families, increased availability of financial aid for students from these families, and shifting cultural attitudes towards higher education. Additionally, economic and educational policies at the national and regional levels could also play a role in this trend.
