World Map
The color range on the world map, from light blue to dark blue, represents the relative number of students enrolled in each country. The color range provides a visual representation of the distribution of students across the globe, with dark blue indicating a higher number of students enrolled and light blue indicating a lower number. By using color to represent the number of students enrolled, this world map offers a simple and effective way of visualizing complex data in an intuitive and engaging manner. It provides a valuable tool for understanding the distribution of students across the globe and for identifying areas where resources and attention may need to be focused to promote equal educational opportunities for all.

Students Attending Public or Private Universities From 1950 - 2020

In our dataset, university were classified as public or private. So we counted the total number of students that attended private and public school every year. On hovering on each of the bar, we can also see the percentage of each group for every year. It looks that the percentage of students attending public schools increased from 36% to 44% from 1950 to 2020. The main reason for this trend is the fact that public colleges are government-funded, making easily accesible by students of every income group; while private schools rely more on tuition and endowments.

Year-wise Trend On Income Groups

The goal of this chart is to visualize the trends in the distribution of students across different income groups over time, on a global scale. This data over a 70-year period (1950 - 2020) highlights changes in the distribution of students across different income groups. It indicates that from 1950 to 2020, the percentage of university students from the upper middle income group has increased, while the percentage from the lower income group has remained low. This trend could be due to a variety of factors such as improved accessibility to education for upper middle income families, increased availability of financial aid for students from these families, and shifting cultural attitudes towards higher education. Additionally, economic and educational policies at the national and regional levels could also play a role in this trend.
